About Coach Chris


Experience & Background

Chris began playing tennis at age 13 and brings over 12 years of experience in competitive play and coaching, including teaching since 2014.

He has competed in both high school and adult leagues and has spent the past five years as a high school varsity tennis coach, helping student-athletes develop strong fundamentals, match strategy, and confidence under real competitive conditions.

As a varsity coach, Chris works closely with players not only on technique, but also on discipline, consistency, and mental toughness — skills that benefit students both on and off the court.


Coaching Philosophy

Chris’s coaching approach is deeply personal. Having navigated much of his own development through independent learning and match experience, he strives to be the coach he once needed — one who provides clear structure, honest feedback, and practical guidance.



Parents and students value his ability to:

Break down complex techniques into easy-to-understand steps
Create structured training plans
Help players see real, measurable improvement
Build confidence and long-term love for the game

His goal is not just better strokes, but stronger, more resilient players.



Player Development & Student Outcomes

Chris has helped students across all levels achieve meaningful milestones in their tennis journeys, including:

Making competitive high school tennis teams
Improving consistency to compete confidently in adult match groups
Supporting college-level players seeking continued growth and competition

Several of his students have continued their academic and tennis paths at respected institutions, including:

Mount San Antonio College (Mt. SAC), Riverside City College (RCC), and the University of Notre Dame.

These outcomes reflect Chris’s commitment to long-term player development, not just short-term improvement.



Who Chris Works With

Chris coaches:

Young children building strong fundamentals
Middle and high school students preparing for team and competition
College players refining technique and match performance
Adult players returning to tennis or aiming to play at a higher level

Whether the goal is making a school team, improving match play, or simply gaining confidence on court, Chris adapts his coaching to each player’s needs.


Lesson Types


Chris offers:

Private one-on-one lessons
Small group training
High school team coaching

Each session is structured, intentional, and focused on progress.


Location

Chris is based in Chino, California, and works with students and families throughout the surrounding communities.
